OJ Simpson TikTok Video Goes Viral With Group Of Young Women Loudly Waking Him

 Posted on June 5, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  503 words  |  Larita Shotwell

TikTok is full of bizarre videos that go viral all the time. A TikTok video of a bunch of young ladies noisily waking up OJ Simpson has the internet perplexed. Andrew Silverman (@baseballcane23) shared the video five days ago. Silverman’s TikTok profile states that he is a former University of Miami baseball player and the proprietor of the sports memorabilia firm Icon Auction & Events, which is situated in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

The captionless TikTok video has received over 459k views and over 15k likes. Furthermore, it has elicited some amusing replies from online users.

OJ Simpson Viral TikTok Video

“Let’s wake up OJ at 1am,” is scrawled over the video. A group of 20 to 30-year-old ladies approach Simpson’s room, where he is seen sleeping, in the footage. They yell and make a lot of noise to wake him up. A guy videotaped the footage, and his voice could be heard stating,

“Let’s go, it’s time to party in the home!” It’s time to get up, let’s get up, let’s get up.”

When a shirtless Simpson awoke and perched on the edge of his bed, all the other ladies burst out laughing and yelling. One lady was observed listening to music on her phone, while another with blonde hair yelled, “Wakey! Wakey!” As the camera shifted to show others using their phones, the entourage fled the room at the conclusion of the film.

The viral video elicited some amusing reactions from internet users, with many referencing the former NFL star’s murder claims. Some even stated their distaste, saying they had a lot of questions about the video. Few people openly termed it “disturbing.”

The former athlete was granted early release from prison in December 2021.

OJ Simpson was sentenced to five years on parole, which was to expire on February 9, 2022. In October 2008, he was convicted of arranging an armed robbery with five other individuals. The gang was accused of harassing two sports memorabilia sellers at Los Angeles’ Palace Station Hotel and Casino. The 74-year-old acknowledged the crime, stating that he took what was once his but had been taken following his 1995 acquittal.
