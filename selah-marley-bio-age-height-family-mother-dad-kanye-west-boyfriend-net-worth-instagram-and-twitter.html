Selah Marley Model

Walking a Chanel show, posing in a Calvin Klein ad, and being a part of Kanye West’s Yeezy lineup would be major accomplishments for any model, but for Selah Marley, the daughter of music legend Lauryn Hill, the achievements are just the beginning. At just 5 feet 3 inches tall, Marley is defying old notions about what petite models can and can’t do, racking up covers and campaigns in quick succession.

It only takes a moment with her on the set of Vogue.com’s New York collections video to see that the 18-year-old radiates confidence and nonchalant cool. In between poses, she talked about what it’s like to go from high school to the glamorous world of modeling, and why she still looks to Mom when it comes to fashion advice.

She’s known she wanted to model since she was a kid.“I was like 11, in sixth grade, and I lived with my dad, so he was staying at the Gansevoort, near Times Square, and I just told him, ‘Daddy, I want to model.’ He was with his girlfriend at the time, who was a model. He said. ‘All right, do the catwalk.’ So I did a little two-step for them!”

This season she got her first taste of life in the front row.“This year I’ve actually been attending a lot of shows. I attended Coach, Michael Kors, Marc Jacobs, and that was dope. I got to do a lot of photo ops and it was a new experience for me because I would walk out and there were paparazzi taking pictures of me. It was cool but definitely new to me.”

Walking for Yeezy Season 5 was the highlight of her New York Fashion Week.“Most of my experience has been doing shoots, so doing Yeezy this season was really awesome. I’m glad that I got that chance; the clothes were amazing. Fashion Week was a little hectic for me, but I’m sure not as bad as models who are doing three shows a day or anything like that.”

Her personal style depends on her mood.“It all depends on how I feel in the morning, so sometimes I’m feeling tomboyish, but I love mixing tomboy with sexy. I’ll wear huge baggy cargo pants and a bikini top or a tank top. I know everyone hates skinny jeans now, but I love them! Sometimes I’ll just wear them with combat boots, or military boots. I love doing a hoodie under a jean jacket, for example, or under a leather jacket.”

Her mom is her biggest fashion influence, but Rihanna is pretty cool, too.“I think my mom for one is incredibly fashionable. She’s the one who exposed me to the fashion world simply because of everything that she has. Our basement is literally like a museum of couture! It’s insane. I can go shopping down there, and I do. I also love Rihanna. I think she’s dope. I love the way she dresses up. I have a million screenshots of her in my phone. If I had to give it to anyone, I’d give it to those two people.”

Working with music icons including Beyoncé for Ivy Park and Kanye West on Yeezy is a special treat.“I’m very honored that these huge icons have chosen me. I feel great that these people who I respect so much admire me and my style.”
